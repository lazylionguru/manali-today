## The Short Answer

Yes, May is a good time to visit Manali. But you need to know what you're walking into. The weather is genuinely pleasant, snow is still hanging around at higher elevations, and the valley is green. The catch is that May sits right at the edge of peak season. The crowds start climbing fast as summer holidays approach, and the chaos I write about in my peak summer survival piece starts kicking in around now. If you come prepared for that, you'll have a fine trip. If you show up expecting empty roads and quiet cafes, you won't.

## What May Is Actually Like

Let me tell you what May looks like on the ground here in Old Manali, where I've been living since 2018.

The weather turns properly good. The snow melts out of the main valley, the apple orchards bloom, and the Beas runs full and cold. Days are warm enough for a t shirt if you're in the sun, but the shade still bites. Mornings and evenings need a proper jacket. You won't need the heavy winter gear you'd bring in January, but don't pack like you're heading to Goa either. According to the [India Meteorological Department](https://mausam.imd.gov.in), Manali's average high in May hovers around 22°C, with cool nights dropping to about 10°C.

Up at Rohtang Pass, the snow is still deep. The pass usually opens to tourist vehicles sometime in May, depending on how much snow fell over winter and how fast the Border Roads Organisation can clear it. Some years it opens early in the month. Some years it stays closed until late May or even June. That uncertainty is part of what I track every day on the site. If Rohtang is your main reason for coming, you should [check live Rohtang Pass status](https://manali.today/is-rohtang-pass-open-today) before you book anything. I update that page myself based on what I see and what my local contacts tell me. I don't run an automated feed.

Down in the valley, the snow is mostly gone from Manali town itself by May. If you want to see and walk on snow without driving up to the pass, you need to head higher. Solang Valley still holds snow through most of May. So do the higher trekking trails. I wrote about one of my favorite spring walks, [the Three Meadows Trail from Rocky's Cafe in Old Manali](https://manali.today/blog/rockys-cafe-meadows-trek-old-manali), which is a good option this time of year if the lower meadows are clear and the higher ones still have patches.

The river is loud in May. The meltwater comes down hard. If you're staying near the Beas, you'll hear it at night. I like that sound. Some people find it keeps them awake. Your call.

## What to Plan Around

### Book Ahead, Seriously

From May onward, accommodation fills up. This is the point in the year where that advice stops being optional. If you show up without a booking in May, especially on a weekend, you will struggle. The cheap guesthouses fill first. The mid range places go next. Even the expensive resorts get booked solid by the third week of the month. I see people every May walking around Old Manali with backpacks looking for a room. Don't be those people.

### Where to Stay Matters More Now

If you've read [the complete Manali travel guide](https://manali.today/guide/manali), you know I recommend staying away from Mall Road if you want peace. In May, that advice starts mattering most. The traffic on Mall Road gets heavy. The honking starts early and ends late. If you're in a guesthouse right on that strip, you'll hear it all.

This is the time to look at Shanag or Burwa. Those are the quieter pockets, slightly outside the main town, closer to the river. You trade a short walk or auto ride to the market for a good night's sleep and a view that isn't someone else's hotel wall. I stay in Old Manali, which has its own noise issues during peak season, but it's a different kind of noise. Cafe music and backpacker chatter instead of bus horns.

### Road Conditions

The roads are mostly clear in May, but that doesn't mean smooth sailing. The Chandigarh to Manali highway sees heavy traffic this month. Long weekends turn the drive into a crawl. If you're driving yourself, leave early. If you're taking a bus, book in advance. The HRTC buses fill up fast.

The Atal Tunnel is running, which cuts hours off the drive from the other side, but it also funnels more traffic into Manali than the old road used to. The tunnel itself is fine in May. No snow issues at that elevation. But the approach roads on both sides can get congested.

### What to Pack

Layers. That's the whole answer. A good fleece or light puffer for mornings and evenings. A rain shell because May gets the occasional pre monsoon shower. Comfortable walking shoes. Sunscreen, because the UV is strong at this altitude even when the air feels cool. Sunglasses. If you plan to go up to Rohtang, bring proper shoes with grip. The snow gets slushy by afternoon and you'll slide around in sneakers.

Don't bring heavy thermals or a big winter parka. You won't need them in the valley. You might want them for Rohtang, but you can rent a jacket and boots up there for cheap. No point carrying that weight.

## Who Should (and Shouldn't) Come in May

### Come in May if

You want good weather without extreme cold. You want to see green valleys with snow capped peaks above. You're okay with moderate crowds and don't mind sharing the trail with other people. You're flexible about Rohtang, happy to go if it's open but not devastated if it isn't. You like river sound at night. You're the kind of traveler who books ahead and plans around conditions rather than hoping everything works out.

You're a first time visitor who wants a safe, comfortable introduction to Manali. May is a gentle month compared to the monsoon or deep winter. It's hard to have a bad time in May if you come prepared.

### Don't come in May if

You want solitude. You want empty roads and silent trails. You want to be the only person at your cafe. May is not that month. Come in February or March for that, or late September after the crowds thin out.

You're on a tight budget and can't book ahead. Prices climb in May. The cheap rooms go first. If you're the type who likes to show up and negotiate, you'll end up paying more than you would have if you just booked online in April.

You're coming specifically for snow in the town itself. The valley floor is green by May. If you want to step out of your hotel and make a snowman, come in January or February. You can [check live snow status](https://manali.today/) to see what conditions are like right now before you decide, but generally speaking, May snow is up high, not down low.

You're dead set on Rohtang and can't handle the uncertainty. Some years the pass opens late. Some years it opens and closes again due to fresh snow or road damage. If a Rohtang trip is the whole point of your vacation, pick a safer month like June or October. The [Himachal Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation](https://himachaltourism.gov.in) provides official updates on pass accessibility during the season.

## Closing

I get asked this question a lot. People type "manali in may" into Google because they're planning a trip and they don't want to mess it up. I understand that. I've been here long enough to watch people arrive at the wrong time with the wrong expectations and leave disappointed. That's not what I want for anyone.

May is a good month. It's not perfect, but no month is. The weather is kind, the valley is beautiful, and the energy of the season starting is something you can feel. Just come with your eyes open. Book your room. Check the pass status. Pack smart. And when you get here, slow down. Don't try to see everything. Sit by the river. Walk through the orchards. Talk to the people who live here year round.

That's the real Manali. May is a fine time to meet her.
