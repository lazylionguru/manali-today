I get asked about this walk more than almost any other short trek around here. People come to Old Manali wanting something easy but real, something that gets them out of the cafe scene and into the hills without committing to a full day. The three meadows trail from Rocky's is exactly that. I've sent dozens of friends up there over the years, and I still walk it myself when I need to clear my head.

Let me tell you how it works.

## The Trail, Start to Finish

Rocky's Cafe sits at the upper end of Old Manali, past the main market stretch, where the road narrows and the guesthouses start thinning out. You know you are in the right spot when you see the cafe on your left and the trailhead directly behind it. No signboard, no ticket booth. Just a dirt path that cuts up into the forest.

The trail heads up through three meadows, one after another, each a little higher and quieter than the last. That is the whole shape of the walk. Three open patches of grass, separated by short stretches of forest, climbing steadily as you go.

**First meadow.** You reach it maybe fifteen minutes from the cafe. It is the largest of the three and the most exposed to the village below. You can still hear the river and the occasional motorbike from here. Locals graze their cattle in this one during summer. The grass is thick, and there is a single apple tree near the upper edge that people always stop under. Keep going straight across, aiming for the treeline on the far side.

**Second meadow.** The forest stretch between the first and second takes about ten minutes. It is mostly deodar and rhododendron, cool and shaded. The path is obvious but narrow in places. When you break out into the second meadow, the village noise drops away completely. This one is smaller, more enclosed by trees. The grass is shorter here, almost like someone keeps it trimmed. A stream runs along the left edge in spring and early summer. The trail curves right along the tree line rather than crossing straight over.

**Third meadow.** The climb from the second to the third is the steepest section of the whole walk. Nothing serious, just a steady uphill through more forest, maybe twenty minutes. The path gets a bit rocky here. Then you step out and the whole sky opens up. The third meadow is small, almost a clearing more than a meadow, but it sits high enough that you get a proper view back over the [Kullu Valley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kullu_Valley). This is where most people stop, eat their snacks, take their photos, and turn around.

Coming back down is straightforward. Same path, reverse direction. The whole round trip takes about two hours at a relaxed pace, maybe three if you really take your time in the meadows.

One thing I should tell you. There is a fork in the trail about five minutes past the first meadow. The right-hand trail instead of left from Rocky's leads toward Goshal village instead, with good stopping points along the way. That is a different walk entirely, longer and steeper, and it comes out on the road above Old Manali. If you want the three meadows, take the left fork. If you take the right, you will end up in Goshal and have to walk back on the road. I have done it. It is fine, but not what you came for.

## What Makes It Worth Doing

Three things, specifically.

First, the progression. Each meadow feels different because of the altitude gain and the change in exposure. The first one is broad and social. The second is enclosed and quiet. The third is exposed and high. You get three distinct moods in under an hour of walking. That is rare for a short trail.

Second, the quiet. The second meadow especially. Once you are past the first one and into the forest stretch, the Manali noise just stops. No music from cafes, no traffic, no construction. Just wind in the deodars and the occasional bird. For people who have been in Old Manali for a few days and are starting to feel the buzz of the village, this walk resets you.

Third, it is genuinely accessible. You do not need to be fit. You do not need hiking boots. You do not need a guide. You just need to start walking from Rocky's and keep going up. I have seen families with small children make it to the second meadow. I have seen sixty-year-old solo travelers make it to the third. It is a trail that rewards effort proportionally without punishing anyone who takes it slow.

## Practical Notes

**Best time of day.** Morning, before 10 AM. The light is better, the meadows are empty, and the afternoon clouds have not rolled in yet. In summer, the afternoon sun hits the third meadow hard with no shade. In winter, you want to be done and heading back down before 3 PM because the temperature drops fast once the sun goes behind the ridge.

**Fitness level.** Easy to moderate. The steepest section is the forest climb between the second and third meadows, but it is short. Anyone who walks regularly in flat terrain can handle it. If you have knee problems, bring a walking stick for the descent. The path can be loose in places.

**What to carry.** Water, definitely. There is no reliable water source after the stream in the second meadow, and that dries up by July. Sunscreen and a hat for the open sections. A light jacket even in summer because the third meadow gets wind. Snacks if you plan to sit at the top. No trash, carry everything back down.

**Wildlife.** You might see cows and goats in the first meadow, sometimes horses. Monkeys in the forest stretches, especially near the second meadow. Do not feed them. They will follow you. I have seen them snatch bags from people who stopped to eat. Keep your food in your pack.

**Phone signal.** Fades in and out. You will have signal in the first meadow, patchy in the second, and generally good again at the third because of the elevation. Do not rely on it.

**Season.** Accessible from April through November. In December through March, snow covers the upper trail and the third meadow can be knee-deep. The locals know the route in snow, but I would not send a visitor up there without proper gear and someone who knows the ground. For current weather conditions in the area, check the [India Meteorological Department forecast for Manali](https://mausam.imd.gov.in/).

## Who Should Skip This

If you are looking for a proper hike with significant elevation gain and a summit view, this is not it. The three meadows trail tops out at maybe 250 meters of climb from the cafe. You are never truly above the treeline. The view is nice but not dramatic.

If you have mobility issues or trouble with uneven ground, the forest sections have roots and loose rocks that can be tricky. The first meadow is easy, but getting to the second and third requires navigating those sections.

If you are short on time and just want a quick look at the valley, walk up to the first meadow and stop there. You will get the same view in less time. The second and third meadows add distance and effort without a radically different payoff.

If it has rained heavily the night before, the trail gets slippery, especially the forest sections. I would skip it and do the walk another day.

That is the walk. Nothing fancy, nothing secret, nothing that needs a guide or a permit or a special app to find. Just a trail that starts behind a cafe and climbs through three open fields into the quiet part of the hills.
