I have been living in Old Manali since 2018. In that time I have walked a lot of trails around this valley. Some are famous. Some should stay quiet. This one, the walk from Jagatsukh up toward Bhanara village and then to Nag Devta temple and Baghi meadow, sits right in the middle. It deserves more attention than it gets, but not too much. You will understand when you get there.

Most people come to Manali and do the same three things. They go to Solang. They stand in line for Rohtang. They walk the mall. That is fine if you want what everyone else wants. But if you asked me where to go for a real afternoon, something that feels like the Manali I actually live in, I would point you to this trail. Before you head out, [check live snow status](https://manali.today/) so you know what the higher elevations look like. The weather changes fast up there.

## The Trail, Start to Finish

The walk starts in Jagatsukh. That is a village about six kilometers from Manali town, past the apple orchards and the old temples. You can get there by local bus or shared taxi. I usually tell people to take a cab from Manali and ask the driver to drop them at the Jagatsukh roadhead where the trail to Bhanara begins.

From Jagatsukh the trail climbs steadily toward Bhanara village. It is not a difficult walk. The path is wide enough in most places, used by locals moving between villages. You will pass through pine forests and open patches where you can see the whole Kullu valley spread out below. The air changes as you gain elevation. It gets thinner and cooler and smells like cedar and dry earth.

Bhanara itself is a small village. Not much happens there. A few houses, some terraced fields, the usual mountain quiet. You walk through it and keep going. The trail narrows after Bhanara. That is when it gets good.

From Bhanara you walk to Nag Devta temple. I have been to a lot of temples in this region. This one is different. It sits tucked into the hillside, half hidden by deodar trees, with a small courtyard and an old stone roof. The locals keep it clean. There are always fresh flowers on the main shrine. The place has a stillness that is hard to find even in the quieter parts of Manali. It is one of the best hidden spots in the valley, and I mean that exactly as I say it. No exaggeration. The temple is dedicated to the serpent deity, a common figure in [Hindu mythology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N%C4%81ga).

Past the temple a little further is a small meadow called Baghi. This is where the trail pays off. Baghi is surrounded by forest on all sides. The grass is short and soft. The trees close in around it like walls. I have sat there for hours watching the light move across the clearing. It is hard to describe how good it is. You have to see it.

The whole walk from Jagatsukh to Baghi takes about two and a half to three hours at a relaxed pace. You can do it faster if you push. I would not recommend that. The point is to move slow and let the place settle into you.

## What Makes It Worth Doing

Most trails around Manali are either too crowded or too punishing. This one is neither. The elevation gain is gentle enough that you do not need to be a mountaineer. The distance is short enough that you can do it as a half day trip and still be back in town for a late lunch.

What makes it special is the sequence. You start in a village with real life happening. You move through a second village that barely registers on any map. You arrive at a temple that feels genuinely sacred, not tourist sacred, actually sacred. And then you end in a meadow that feels like it was placed there specifically for people who need a break from everything.

Baghi meadow is the real reward. The forest enclosure makes it feel private even when there are other people around. I have been there on summer afternoons with no one else in sight. The silence is complete except for wind and birds. If you lie down in the grass and close your eyes, you can almost forget there is a town with traffic and noise and bad souvenir shops an hour downhill.

I also like this trail because it connects to the larger network of paths that go deeper into the mountains. From Baghi you can continue higher if you have the time and energy. But the meadow itself is enough for most people.

## Practical Notes

Let me be direct about the safety part. Do not wander this area after sunset. There are bears around. I have never had a problem myself, but I have heard stories from locals. Bears are not aggressive unless surprised or provoked. Walking through their territory in the dark is asking for trouble. Start early. Be back in Bhanara or Jagatsukh before the light starts to fade.

The best time of day for this trail is morning. Start by 8 AM. You will have the path to yourself, the light will be soft, and you will be back down before the afternoon heat hits. In summer the meadow gets warm by midday. In winter the trail can be snowy and slippery. [Check live Rohtang Pass status](https://manali.today/is-rohtang-pass-open-today) before you go if you are planning anything that connects to higher elevations, though for this trail specifically the road conditions in Manali town matter more.

Fitness level needed is moderate. If you can walk uphill for an hour without stopping, you are fine. There is nothing technical. No scrambling, no exposure, no steep drops. Good walking shoes help. Trekking poles are optional. Bring water and some food. There is nowhere to buy supplies once you leave Jagatsukh.

## Who Should Skip This

Be honest with yourself. If you do not like walking on dirt paths for more than an hour, skip it. If you need a proper viewpoint with a photo platform and a chai stall, skip it. If you are traveling with very young children or anyone who cannot handle uneven ground, skip it. The meadow is beautiful but it is not accessible. There is no road to Baghi. That is the whole point.

Also skip it if you are in a hurry. This is not a trail to rush through. If your idea of a good hike is covering maximum distance in minimum time, go do Hampta Pass or something. This walk rewards people who stop and sit and look.

The same goes if you are uncomfortable with solitude. The trail is quiet. Nag Devta temple is quiet. Baghi meadow is very quiet. Some people find that unsettling. If you need crowds and noise to feel safe, stay in Manali town.

## What I Would Tell a Friend

I have sent a lot of people on this walk over the years. Not one has come back disappointed. The ones who went early and came back late, those are the ones who found something in Baghi that they did not expect. A kind of peace that does not come easily in most places.

That is the real reason I keep writing about trails like this. Not for clicks or lists or travel awards. Because I live here. Because I know what is good. And because when you come to Manali and ask me for help, I want you to see the valley the way I see it. Not through a bus window. Not from a hotel balcony. On your own feet, moving slow, with a meadow waiting at the end.
